SURVIVAL MANUAL

The Rule of Threes

You can survive roughly:

  • 3 Minutes without breathable air.
  • 3 Hours without shelter in extreme conditions.
  • 3 Days without water.
  • 3 Weeks without food.
Fire Building

1. Tinder: Dry, hair-like material (dry grass, inner bark, pocket lint) that takes a spark.

2. Kindling: Twigs ranging from pencil-lead to thumb thickness. Must "snap" when broken.

3. Fuel: Wrist-thick or larger branches to sustain the fire.

Tip: Build a "teepee" structure around tinder to allow oxygen flow.

Immediate Shelter

Debris Hut:

  1. Find a sturdy ridgepole (strong branch) and prop one end on a stump or rock.
  2. Lay side branches (ribs) along the ridgepole.
  3. Pile 2-3 feet of dry leaves/debris on top for insulation.
  4. Fill the inside with a thick bed of dry leaves to insulate yourself from the cold ground.
Water Purification

1. Boil: Bring to rolling boil for 1 full minute (3 minutes at high altitude).

2. Solar Still: Dig a hole, place container in center, cover hole with plastic sheet, place small rock over sheet center directly above container. Sun evaporates soil moisture into the cup.

Universal Edibility Test

WARNING: Test only one part of one plant at a time. Fast for 8 hours prior.

1. Contact: Rub crushed plant on inner wrist/elbow. Wait 15 mins. If reaction occurs, discard.

2. Lip: Place small amount on outer lip for 3 mins.

3. Mouth: Chew small portion, hold in mouth 15 mins WITHOUT swallowing.

4. Swallow: Swallow small amount, wait 8 hours. If no ill effects, eat a small handful and wait 8 more hours. If safe, it's edible.
